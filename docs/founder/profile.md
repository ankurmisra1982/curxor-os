## The viral moment

I've carried a single thought since OpenClaw went viral a few months back: **this shouldn't be a weekend project you duct-tape together.** It should ship out of the box — local inference, agent loops, skills, bridges, a command surface you own. One appliance. Plug it in. Mint your Claws. No assembly required.

Then the news hit.

SpaceX went public. Within a day or two, they exercised their option to acquire Cursor. The whole timeline lit up — frontier AI, sovereign compute, who owns the cognitive layer. Everyone had an opinion. I had a spec.

On **June 18, 2026**, I posted a thread tagging @elonmusk, @cursor_ai, and @SpaceX — not to chase hype, but to name the product the ecosystem still doesn't ship:

> A sovereign edge box serving as your personal server and codebase.  
> A centralized cognitive hub orchestrating a localized AI ecosystem.

That wasn't a pitch deck slide. That was the product brief.

Within hours I changed my name, bio, and header to **CurXor HQ**. Not "thinking about it." Not "DM me if interested." Public commitment.

**About twenty-four hours later, I started building.**

Not because the acquisition created the idea — I'd already known what was missing. Because the moment made it undeniable: the world is ready for a box, and nobody is shipping it.

---

## What I'm building

**CurXor** is that box.

A sovereign edge appliance on bare metal — local LLM, OpenClaw engine, telemetry mesh, Flight Command dashboard. Ten Claws and The Forge. Capital, content, outreach, vital, kin — digital employees that run 24/7 on your metal. Outbound trades and posts egress through a controlled bridge; the cognitive core stays local.

**$3,999 once. $0/mo API rent.** Alpha on your LAN. Sovereignty by design.

The tweet was the public launch. The repo is the proof.

## Mission

Make autonomous AI **ownable** — not rented, not hosted, not locked inside someone else's cloud.

Ship the full stack the way OpenClaw proved people want it: **working, out of the box, on hardware you control.**
